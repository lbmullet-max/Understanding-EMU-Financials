FAN vs. Bill — What’s the Difference?

EMU produces two separate documents. Understanding which is which helps you know where to look and what the numbers mean.

Key insight: The FAN is a planning tool showing estimated figures for both semesters. The Bill shows actual charges for one semester at a time.

What It Shows
  • Estimated costs/charges & financial aid awards
  • Covers fall AND spring semesters
  • Does not include course fees
  • Does not include health/secondary athletic insurance
  • Actual charges for tuition, housing & meal plan
  • One semester only
  • Includes course fees based on registered classes
  • Includes health/secondary athletic insurance
Financial Aid Awards
  • Estimated amounts — may show as “pending” on myEMU
  • Awards finalized & disbursed early each semester
  • Award amounts can be adjusted
  • Estimated (pending) until funds transferred from Fin Aid Office
  • Disbursed awards show as “posted,” “history,” or “current”
  • Adjustment amounts show as a debit or credit
Federal Work Study / EMU Work Program
  • Included as a financial aid award (potential earnings)
  • Student paid directly for hours worked (biweekly)
  • Not included as a financial aid award credit
  • FWS/EWP payment is not disbursed to the student’s account
Federal Student Loans
  • Eligible amounts awarded for each loan type
  • Loans certified when all needed forms received
  • Student notified when disbursement is received
  • Eligible loan awards included as credits until declined
  • Loan awards removed from account when declined
  • Disbursed funds reduced by origination fees
  • Funds credited directly to student’s account
Federal Parent PLUS Loans
  • Eligibility based on COA minus awarded financial aid
  • Parent can borrow all or some of eligible amount
  • Contact Fin Aid Office for a larger PLUS amount
  • Certified after approved application & MPN received
  • Disbursed after certification (parent will be notified)
  • Note: Balance owed is not the parent loan eligibility amount
  • Balance owed may include charges not on FAN
  • Loan disbursement reduced by origination fees
  • Funds credited directly to student’s account

Section 3

Your First Bill (Statement of Account)

Available on myEMU after July 1, your Bill shows actual charges and any financial aid credits applied to your account for that semester.

Account Summary (top right)

Shows your current balance, previous balance, and preferred payment method. The amount shown is what is due.

Previous Account Balance

Any balance carried from a prior period. Should be $.00 for incoming students.

Current Activity

All charges and financial aid credits posted this semester. Pending aid appears here as a credit when disbursed.

Current Account Balance

Your total amount owed after all credits. A dash (–) in front means you have a credit balance.
